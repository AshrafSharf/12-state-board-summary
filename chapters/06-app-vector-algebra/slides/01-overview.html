6   Applications of Vector Algebra — Overview

What This Chapter Covers

This chapter applies vector algebra to 3-dimensional geometry — computing volumes via scalar triple products, simplifying expressions with vector triple products, and deriving equations of lines and planes in multiple forms (parametric, non-parametric, Cartesian).

Why It Matters for Exams

Exam questions heavily test:

  • Shortest distance between skew and parallel lines
  • Angle computations between lines, between planes, and between a line and a plane
  • Plane equations through various conditions (3 points, normal + point, intercepts)
  • Image of a point in a plane and foot of perpendicular
  • Coplanarity checks for vectors and lines
