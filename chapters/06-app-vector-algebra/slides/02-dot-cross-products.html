6.1–6.2   Scalar Product, Vector Product & Geometry

Core Idea

The dot product $\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = |\vec{a}||\vec{b}|\cos\theta$ measures projection (result is a scalar). The cross product $\vec{a} \times \vec{b}$ gives a vector perpendicular to both with magnitude $|\vec{a}||\vec{b}|\sin\theta$ (result is a vector).

$$\boxed{\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = a_1b_1 + a_2b_2 + a_3b_3}$$
$$\boxed{\vec{a} \times \vec{b} = \begin{vmatrix} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\ a_1 & a_2 & a_3 \\ b_1 & b_2 & b_3 \end{vmatrix}}$$
$$\boxed{\vec{a} \times \vec{b} = \begin{vmatrix} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\ a_1 & a_2 & a_3 \\ b_1 & b_2 & b_3 \end{vmatrix}}$$

Visual: Dot Product vs Cross Product

Dot product = projection of $\vec{b}$ onto $\vec{a}$ times $|\vec{a}|$
Result: scalar

Cross product = perpendicular vector
Magnitude = parallelogram area. Result: vector

Supporting Formulas

$$\theta = \cos^{-1}\!\left(\frac{\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b}}{|\vec{a}||\vec{b}|}\right)$$
$$|\vec{a} \times \vec{b}| = |\vec{a}||\vec{b}|\sin\theta$$
$$\text{Area of } \triangle ABC = \tfrac{1}{2}|\overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AC}|$$
$$W = \vec{F} \cdot \vec{d}$$Work done
$$\vec{\tau} = \vec{r} \times \vec{F}$$Torque

Spot the Clue

  • "Prove by vector method" with trig identities like $\cos(\alpha+\beta)$, sine rule, cosine rule
  • Force + displacement given together → work = dot product
  • Force at a point with position vector → torque = cross product
  • "Find the angle between" two vectors → use $\cos^{-1}$

Ex: Total Work Done (Ex 6.9)

Forces $2\hat{i}+5\hat{j}+6\hat{k}$ and $-\hat{i}-2\hat{j}-\hat{k}$ displace a particle from $(4,-3,-2)$ to $(6,1,-3)$.

Step 1: $\vec{F} = (2-1)\hat{i}+(5-2)\hat{j}+(6-1)\hat{k} = \hat{i}+3\hat{j}+5\hat{k}$

Step 2: $\vec{d} = (6-4)\hat{i}+(1+3)\hat{j}+(-3+2)\hat{k} = 2\hat{i}+4\hat{j}-\hat{k}$

$$W = \vec{F} \cdot \vec{d} = 2+12-5 = 9$$
$$\boxed{W = 9 \text{ units}}$$

Watch Out

$$\vec{a} \times \vec{b} = \vec{b} \times \vec{a}$$ Cross product is NOT commutative
$$\vec{a} \times \vec{b} = -(\vec{b} \times \vec{a})$$ Anti-commutative: swap = flip sign

Also remember: $\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b}$ is a scalar, $\vec{a} \times \vec{b}$ is a vector. Don't mix them up.
