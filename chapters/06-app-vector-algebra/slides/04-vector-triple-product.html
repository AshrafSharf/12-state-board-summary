6.4   Vector Triple Product & Identities

Core Idea

The vector triple product $\vec{a} \times (\vec{b} \times \vec{c})$ is a vector that lies in the plane of $\vec{b}$ and $\vec{c}$. It is NOT associative. The expansion follows the BAC-CAB rule.

$$\boxed{\vec{a} \times (\vec{b} \times \vec{c}) = (\vec{a} \cdot \vec{c})\vec{b} - (\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b})\vec{c}}$$
$$\boxed{\begin{aligned} &\vec{a} \times (\vec{b} \times \vec{c}) \\ &= (\vec{a} \cdot \vec{c})\vec{b} - (\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b})\vec{c} \end{aligned}}$$

Related Identities

$$(\vec{a} \times \vec{b}) \times \vec{c} = (\vec{a} \cdot \vec{c})\vec{b} - (\vec{b} \cdot \vec{c})\vec{a}$$
$$\begin{aligned} &(\vec{a} \times \vec{b}) \times \vec{c} \\ &= (\vec{a} \cdot \vec{c})\vec{b} - (\vec{b} \cdot \vec{c})\vec{a} \end{aligned}$$
$$\text{Jacobi: } \vec{a} \times (\vec{b} \times \vec{c}) + \vec{b} \times (\vec{c} \times \vec{a}) + \vec{c} \times (\vec{a} \times \vec{b}) = \vec{0}$$
$$\begin{aligned} &\vec{a} \times (\vec{b} \times \vec{c}) + \vec{b} \times (\vec{c} \times \vec{a}) \\ &+ \vec{c} \times (\vec{a} \times \vec{b}) = \vec{0} \end{aligned}$$
$$\text{Lagrange: } (\vec{a} \times \vec{b}) \cdot (\vec{c} \times \vec{d}) = \begin{vmatrix} \vec{a} \cdot \vec{c} & \vec{a} \cdot \vec{d} \\ \vec{b} \cdot \vec{c} & \vec{b} \cdot \vec{d} \end{vmatrix}$$
$$(\vec{a} \times \vec{b}) \cdot (\vec{c} \times \vec{d}) = \begin{vmatrix} \vec{a}\!\cdot\!\vec{c} & \vec{a}\!\cdot\!\vec{d} \\ \vec{b}\!\cdot\!\vec{c} & \vec{b}\!\cdot\!\vec{d} \end{vmatrix}$$
$$[\vec{a} \times \vec{b},\; \vec{b} \times \vec{c},\; \vec{c} \times \vec{a}] = [\vec{a}, \vec{b}, \vec{c}]^2$$

Spot the Clue

  • Nested cross products like $\vec{a} \times (\vec{b} \times \vec{c})$ → expand using BAC-CAB
  • "Prove non-associativity" → compute both sides separately and show they differ
  • Middle vector rule: result is a linear combination of the vectors INSIDE the inner bracket
  • Product of two cross products dotted together → Lagrange's identity

Ex: Verify Non-Associativity (Ex 6.22)

$\vec{a}=-2\hat{i}+3\hat{j}-2\hat{k}$, $\vec{b}=3\hat{i}-\hat{j}+3\hat{k}$, $\vec{c}=2\hat{i}-5\hat{j}+\hat{k}$

Step 1: $\vec{a} \times \vec{b} = 7\hat{i}+0\hat{j}-7\hat{k}$

Step 2: $(\vec{a} \times \vec{b}) \times \vec{c} = -35\hat{i}-21\hat{j}-35\hat{k}$

Step 3: $\vec{b} \times \vec{c} = 14\hat{i}+3\hat{j}-13\hat{k}$

Step 4: $\vec{a} \times (\vec{b} \times \vec{c}) = -33\hat{i}-54\hat{j}-48\hat{k}$

$$\boxed{(\vec{a} \times \vec{b}) \times \vec{c} = -35\hat{i}-21\hat{j}-35\hat{k} \neq -33\hat{i}-54\hat{j}-48\hat{k} = \vec{a} \times (\vec{b} \times \vec{c})}$$
$$\boxed{\begin{aligned} &(\vec{a}\!\times\!\vec{b})\!\times\!\vec{c} = -35\hat{i}\!-\!21\hat{j}\!-\!35\hat{k} \\ &\neq -33\hat{i}\!-\!54\hat{j}\!-\!48\hat{k} = \vec{a}\!\times\!(\vec{b}\!\times\!\vec{c}) \end{aligned}}$$

Watch Out

$$\vec{a} \times (\vec{b} \times \vec{c}) = (\vec{a} \times \vec{b}) \times \vec{c}$$ NOT associative in general!
$$\vec{a} \times (\vec{b} \times \vec{c}) \neq (\vec{a} \times \vec{b}) \times \vec{c}$$ Always expand each side separately
$$\begin{aligned} &\vec{a} \times (\vec{b} \times \vec{c}) \\ &= (\vec{a}\!\cdot\!\vec{c})\vec{a} - (\vec{a}\!\cdot\!\vec{b})\vec{a} \end{aligned}$$ Wrong vectors in result!
$$\begin{aligned} &\vec{a} \times (\vec{b} \times \vec{c}) \\ &= (\vec{a}\!\cdot\!\vec{c})\vec{b} - (\vec{a}\!\cdot\!\vec{b})\vec{c} \end{aligned}$$ Result uses $\vec{b}$ and $\vec{c}$ (inner bracket vectors)
