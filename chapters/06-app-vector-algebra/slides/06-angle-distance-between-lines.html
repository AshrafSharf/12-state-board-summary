6.6   Angle, Intersection & Shortest Distance

Core Idea

The angle between two lines equals the angle between their direction vectors. Two lines in 3D can be intersecting, parallel, or skew. Skew lines are non-parallel and non-intersecting; their shortest distance is measured along the common perpendicular.

$$\boxed{\cos\theta = \frac{|\vec{b} \cdot \vec{d}|}{|\vec{b}||\vec{d}|}}$$

Visual: Skew Lines vs Parallel Lines

Common perpendicular = shortest path
$\delta = \dfrac{|(\vec{c}-\vec{a})\cdot(\vec{b}\times\vec{d})|}{|\vec{b}\times\vec{d}|}$

Perpendicular from any point works
$d = \dfrac{|(\vec{c}-\vec{a})\times\vec{b}|}{|\vec{b}|}$

Distance Formulas

$$\boxed{\text{Skew lines: } \delta = \frac{|(\vec{c}-\vec{a}) \cdot (\vec{b} \times \vec{d})|}{|\vec{b} \times \vec{d}|}}$$
$$\boxed{\delta_{\text{skew}} = \frac{|(\vec{c}\!-\!\vec{a}) \cdot (\vec{b} \!\times\! \vec{d})|}{|\vec{b} \!\times\! \vec{d}|}}$$
$$\boxed{\text{Parallel lines: } d = \frac{|(\vec{c}-\vec{a}) \times \vec{b}|}{|\vec{b}|}}$$

Relationship Summary

RelationshipTestFormula
Parallel$\vec{b} = \lambda\vec{d}$$d = \frac{|(\vec{c}-\vec{a}) \times \vec{b}|}{|\vec{b}|}$
Perpendicular$\vec{b} \cdot \vec{d} = 0$Angle $= \frac{\pi}{2}$
Intersecting$(\vec{c}\!-\!\vec{a}) \cdot (\vec{b} \!\times\! \vec{d}) = 0$Distance $= 0$
Skew$(\vec{c}\!-\!\vec{a}) \cdot (\vec{b} \!\times\! \vec{d}) \neq 0$Use skew distance formula

Spot the Clue

  • "Find shortest distance between two lines" → check parallel or skew first
  • "Show that the lines intersect" → show shortest distance = 0 (scalar triple product in numerator = 0)
  • "Find point of intersection" → equate parametric forms, solve for $s$ and $t$, verify with third equation

Ex: Distance Between Parallel Lines (Ex 6.36)

$\vec{r} = (2\hat{i}+3\hat{j}+4\hat{k}) + t(-2\hat{i}+\hat{j}-2\hat{k})$ and $\frac{x-3}{2} = \frac{y}{-1} = \frac{z+2}{2}$

Step 1: Second line: $\vec{c} = 3\hat{i}-2\hat{k}$, $\vec{d} = 2\hat{i}-\hat{j}+2\hat{k}$. Since $\vec{b} = -\vec{d}$, lines are parallel.

Step 2: $\vec{c}-\vec{a} = \hat{i}-3\hat{j}-6\hat{k}$

$$(\vec{c}-\vec{a}) \times \vec{b} = \begin{vmatrix} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\ 1 & -3 & -6 \\ -2 & 1 & -2 \end{vmatrix} = 12\hat{i}+14\hat{j}-5\hat{k}$$
$$(\vec{c}\!-\!\vec{a}) \times \vec{b} = 12\hat{i}+14\hat{j}-5\hat{k}$$
$$\boxed{d = \frac{\sqrt{144+196+25}}{\sqrt{4+1+4}} = \frac{\sqrt{365}}{3}}$$

Watch Out

$$\text{Parallel lines: use } \frac{|(\vec{c}-\vec{a}) \cdot (\vec{b} \times \vec{d})|}{|\vec{b} \times \vec{d}|}$$ $\vec{b} \times \vec{d} = \vec{0}$ for parallel lines — division by zero!
$$\text{Parallel lines: use } \frac{|(\vec{c}-\vec{a}) \times \vec{b}|}{|\vec{b}|}$$ Correct formula for parallel lines

Trap: The angle formula uses $|\vec{b} \cdot \vec{d}|$ (absolute value) to ensure the acute angle. When checking intersection, verify $s$ and $t$ satisfy ALL THREE coordinate equations.
