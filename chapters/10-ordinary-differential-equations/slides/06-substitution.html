10.6.2   Substitution Method

Spot the Clue

The RHS is a function of one single linear combination $ax+by+c$.

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = f\!\Big(\;\underbrace{ax+by+c}_{\substack{\text{x and y appear ONLY} \\ \text{inside this combo}}}\;\Big)$$

Put $z = ax + by + c \;\Rightarrow\; \dfrac{dy}{dx} = \dfrac{1}{b}\!\left(\dfrac{dz}{dx}-a\right)$. Now it's separable in $z, x$.

The Key Test: Is Everything Inside One $(ax+by+c)$?

$$y' = \sin^2\!\underbrace{(x-y+1)}_{\text{one combo}}$$ $a\!=\!1, b\!=\!{-1}, c\!=\!1$. Substitution!
$$y' = \underbrace{(3x+y+4)}_{a=3,\;b=1,\;c=4}\!^{2}$$ All of $x,y$ inside one combo. Substitution!

$y' = \sin^2(x-y+1)$

$$z=x\!-\!y\!+\!1,\;\frac{dz}{dx}=\cos^2 z$$
$$\boxed{\tan(x\!-\!y\!+\!1)=x+c}$$

$y' = (3x+y+4)^2$

$$z=3x\!+\!y\!+\!4,\; z'=z^2\!+\!3$$
$$\boxed{\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\tan^{-1}\!\!\left(\frac{3x+y+4}{\sqrt{3}}\right)=x+c}$$

Can't Use Substitution When...

$x$ and $y$ appear separately (not locked inside one combo), or in a ratio.

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{\overbrace{x+y}^{\text{combo}}}{\underbrace{x-y}_{\text{different combo}}}$$ Two different combos in a ratio. → Homogeneous ($y=vx$)
$$\frac{dy}{dx} = \underbrace{x^2}_{\text{alone}} + \underbrace{\sin(x+y)}_{\text{combo}}$$ $x^2$ sits outside the combo. Substitution won't clean up.
