10.7   First Order Linear DEs

Spot the Clue

$y$ and $y'$ appear to 1st power only — no $y^2$, no $yy'$, no $(y')^2$, no $\sin y$.

$$\frac{dy}{dx} + \underbrace{P(x)}_{\text{coeff of }y}\!\cdot y = \underbrace{Q(x)}_{\text{RHS: only }x}$$

Divide everything by the coefficient of $y'$ first to get this standard form.

Standard Form & Master Formula

$$\text{I.F.} = e^{\int P(x)\,dx}$$
↓ multiply both sides by I.F.
$$\boxed{y \cdot \underbrace{e^{\int P\,dx}}_{\text{I.F.}} = \int Q \cdot \underbrace{e^{\int P\,dx}}_{\text{I.F.}}\,dx + c}$$

Shorthand: $y \times \text{I.F.} = \displaystyle\int Q \times \text{I.F.}\;dx + c$

Spotting Linear Under Disguise

Often the equation doesn't look like $y' + Py = Q$ until you divide or rearrange.

$$\underbrace{(1+x^3)\frac{dy}{dx}+6x^2 y}_{\text{doesn't look standard}} = 1+x^2$$
↓ divide by $(1+x^3)$
$$\frac{dy}{dx}+\underbrace{\frac{6x^2}{1+x^3}}_{P(x)}\,y = \underbrace{\frac{1+x^2}{1+x^3}}_{Q(x)} \quad\checkmark$$

Alternate: $x$ as Dependent Variable

If solving for $y$ is awkward, try swapping roles:

$$\frac{dx}{dy}+P(y)\,x=Q(y) \;\;\Rightarrow\;\; \text{I.F.}=e^{\int P\,dy}$$
$$\boxed{x \cdot e^{\int P\,dy} = \int Q\,e^{\int P\,dy}\,dy + c}$$

$\dfrac{dy}{dx}+2y=e^{-x}$

$$P\!=\!2,\;\text{I.F.}\!=\!e^{2x}$$
$$ye^{2x}=e^x+c$$
$$\boxed{y=e^{-x}+ce^{-2x}}$$

$\dfrac{dy}{dx}+2y\cot x=3x^2\csc^2 x$

$$P=2\cot x,\;\text{I.F.}=\sin^2 x$$
$$\boxed{y\sin^2 x=x^3+c}$$

$(1\!+\!x^3)\dfrac{dy}{dx}+6x^2y=1\!+\!x^2$

$$P=\frac{6x^2}{1+x^3},\;\text{I.F.}=(1\!+\!x^3)^2$$

$ye^y\,dx=(y^3\!+\!2xe^y)dy$

$$\frac{dx}{dy}-\frac{2}{y}x=y^2 e^{-y}$$
$$\text{I.F.}=\frac{1}{y^2}$$
$$\boxed{x=-y^2 e^{-y}+cy^2}$$

Can't Use Linear I.F. When...

$y$ appears to a power other than 1, or $y$ and $y'$ are multiplied together.

$$\frac{dy}{dx} + \underbrace{y^2}_{\text{power 2!}} = x$$ $y^2$ breaks linearity. This is a Riccati equation — not solvable by I.F.
$$\underbrace{y\frac{dy}{dx}}_{\text{product }y\!\cdot\!y'} + 2x = 0$$ Product $yy'$. But $y\,dy = -2x\,dx$ — it's actually Separable!
