9.9   Volume of Revolution — Disk Method

The Core Idea

Rotate a plane region about an axis. Each thin slice is a disk of radius $r$ and thickness $dx$ (or $dy$). Sum up disk volumes: $\pi r^2\cdot\text{thickness}$.

Revolve about $x$-axis

$$\boxed{V = \pi\int_a^b y^2\,dx}$$

Use when: curve is $y=f(x)$, region lies above $x$-axis, revolving about $x$-axis.

Radius of disk = $y$, thickness = $dx$

Revolve about $y$-axis

$$\boxed{V = \pi\int_c^d x^2\,dy}$$

Use when: curve is $x=g(y)$, region lies to the right of $y$-axis, revolving about $y$-axis.

Radius of disk = $x$, thickness = $dy$

Decision: Which Formula?

  • Axis of revolution = $x$-axis → Integrate w.r.t. $x$, use $\pi\int y^2\,dx$
  • Axis of revolution = $y$-axis → Rewrite curve as $x=g(y)$, integrate w.r.t. $y$, use $\pi\int x^2\,dy$
🟢

Sphere

$y=\sqrt{a^2-x^2}$, about $x$-axis

$$V = \pi\int_{-a}^{a}(a^2-x^2)\,dx$$
$$= \boxed{\frac{4}{3}\pi a^3}$$
🟡

Cone

$y=\frac{r}{h}x$, about $x$-axis

$$V = \pi\int_0^h\frac{r^2}{h^2}x^2\,dx$$
$$= \boxed{\frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h}$$
🟣

Ellipsoid

$\frac{x^2}{a^2}+\frac{y^2}{b^2}=1$, about $x$-axis

$$V = \pi\int_{-a}^{a}\frac{b^2}{a^2}(a^2-x^2)\,dx$$
$$= \boxed{\frac{4}{3}\pi a b^2}$$

Ex: $y=x^2$ about $x$-axis, $0\leq x\leq 1$

$$V = \pi\int_0^1(x^2)^2\,dx = \pi\int_0^1 x^4\,dx = \frac{\pi}{5}$$

Ex: $x=y^2+1$ about $y$-axis, $y\in[-1,1]$

$$V = \pi\int_{-1}^{1}(y^2+1)^2\,dy = 2\pi\int_0^1(y^4+2y^2+1)\,dy$$
$$= 2\pi\left(\frac{1}{5}+\frac{2}{3}+1\right) = \frac{56}{15}\pi$$

Spherical Cap: Don't Forget the Shifted Limits!

A cap of height $h$ cut from a sphere of radius $r$: integrate from $r-h$ to $r$, not from $0$.

$$V_{\text{cap}} = \pi\int_{r-h}^{r}(r^2-x^2)\,dx = \frac{1}{3}\pi h^2(3r-h)$$
