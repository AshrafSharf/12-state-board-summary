9.6   Reduction Formulae — Wallis & Friends

The Big Idea

Express $I_n$ in terms of $I_{n-2}$ (or $I_{n-1}$). Keep reducing until you reach a known base case ($I_0$ or $I_1$).

Trig Power Strategy: Odd vs Even Exponent

Before reaching for Wallis, know the general strategy for $\int\sin^n x\,dx$ or $\int\cos^n x\,dx$:

Odd Power → Split + Pythagorean

Peel off one factor, convert the rest using $\sin^2\!=\!1\!-\!\cos^2$, then u-sub.

$$\int\!\cos^3\!x\,dx = \int\!\cos x(1\!-\!\sin^2\!x)\,dx$$

Let $u=\sin x$, $du=\cos x\,dx$ → $\int(1-u^2)\,du = u-\frac{u^3}{3}+C$

Even Power → Half-Angle Identity

Use power reduction: $\sin^2\!x = \frac{1-\cos 2x}{2}$,   $\cos^2\!x = \frac{1+\cos 2x}{2}$

$$\int\!\sin^2\!x\,dx = \frac{1}{2}\!\int\!(1\!-\!\cos 2x)\,dx$$

$= \frac{x}{2}-\frac{\sin 2x}{4}+C$. For $\sin^4\!x$, apply twice.

Wallis' Formula: $\displaystyle\int_0^{\pi/2}\sin^n x\,dx = \int_0^{\pi/2}\cos^n x\,dx$

$$\boxed{I_n = \frac{n-1}{n}\cdot I_{n-2}}$$

$n$ even: $2,4,6,\ldots$

$$\frac{n\!-\!1}{n}\cdot\frac{n\!-\!3}{n\!-\!2}\cdots\frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{\pi}{2}$$

Base case $I_0=\frac{\pi}{2}$

$n$ odd: $3,5,7,\ldots$

$$\frac{n\!-\!1}{n}\cdot\frac{n\!-\!3}{n\!-\!2}\cdots\frac{2}{3}\cdot 1$$

Base case $I_1=1$

Quick Examples

$$\int_0^{\pi/2}\sin^5 x\,dx = \frac{4}{5}\cdot\frac{2}{3}\cdot 1 = \frac{8}{15}$$ Odd: ends at $1$
$$\int_0^{\pi/2}\cos^6 x\,dx = \frac{5}{6}\cdot\frac{3}{4}\cdot\frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{\pi}{2} = \frac{5\pi}{32}$$ Even: ends at $\frac{\pi}{2}$
$$\begin{aligned} \int_0^{\pi/2}\!\cos^6 x\,dx &= \tfrac{5}{6}\!\cdot\!\tfrac{3}{4}\!\cdot\!\tfrac{1}{2}\!\cdot\!\tfrac{\pi}{2} \\ &= \tfrac{5\pi}{32} \end{aligned}$$ Even: ends at $\frac{\pi}{2}$

$\displaystyle\int_0^{\pi/2}\sin^m x\cos^n x\,dx$  —  Mixed Powers

$$I_{m,n} = \frac{n-1}{m+n}\cdot I_{m,n-2}$$

Both $m,n$ even

$$\frac{(n\!-\!1)(n\!-\!3)\cdots 1}{(m\!+\!n)(m\!+\!n\!-\!2)\cdots}\cdot\frac{(m\!-\!1)(m\!-\!3)\cdots 1}{m(m\!-\!2)\cdots 2}\cdot\frac{\pi}{2}$$
$$\begin{aligned} &\frac{(n\!-\!1)(n\!-\!3)\cdots 1}{(m\!+\!n)(m\!+\!n\!-\!2)\cdots} \\[4pt] &\;\cdot\frac{(m\!-\!1)(m\!-\!3)\cdots 1}{m(m\!-\!2)\cdots 2}\cdot\frac{\pi}{2} \end{aligned}$$

$n$ odd (or $m$ odd)

$$\frac{(n\!-\!1)(n\!-\!3)\cdots 2}{(m\!+\!n)(m\!+\!n\!-\!2)\cdots}\cdot\frac{1}{(m\!+\!1)}$$
$$\frac{(n\!-\!1)(n\!-\!3)\cdots 2}{(m\!+\!n)(m\!+\!n\!-\!2)\cdots}\cdot\frac{1}{m\!+\!1}$$

No $\frac{\pi}{2}$ at the end!

Beta Integral: $\displaystyle\int_0^1 x^m(1-x)^n\,dx = \frac{m!\cdot n!}{(m+n+1)!}$

$$I_{m,n} = \frac{n}{m+n+1}\cdot I_{m,n-1} \quad\text{or}\quad \frac{m!\cdot n!}{(m+n+1)!}$$
$$\begin{aligned} I_{m,n} &= \frac{n}{m+n+1}\cdot I_{m,n-1} \\ &\text{or}\quad \frac{m!\cdot n!}{(m+n+1)!} \end{aligned}$$
$$\int_0^1 x^3(1-x)^4\,dx = \frac{3!\cdot 4!}{8!} = \frac{6\times 24}{40320} = \frac{1}{280}$$
$$\begin{aligned} \int_0^1 x^3(1\!-\!x)^4\,dx &= \frac{3!\cdot 4!}{8!} \\ &= \frac{6\times 24}{40320} = \frac{1}{280} \end{aligned}$$

Tip: Converting to Wallis Form

If you see $\int_0^{2a}x^2\sqrt{2ax-x^2}\,dx$ or similar, substitute $x = 2a\sin^2\theta$ (or $x=a\cos^2\theta$) to convert to $\sin^m\theta\cos^n\theta$ form, then apply reduction formulae.
