Trig Substitution — The Right Triangle That Hides Inside

Every trig substitution comes from a right triangle. Place $\theta$ at one vertex. The Pythagorean theorem connects the three sides — and one side is your square root!

$\sqrt{a^2 - x^2}$

$$x = a\sin\theta, \quad dx = a\cos\theta\,d\theta$$

$\sin\theta = \dfrac{x}{a}$  →  $\theta = \sin^{-1}\!\!\left(\dfrac{x}{a}\right)$

$a^2 - a^2\sin^2\!\theta = a^2\cos^2\!\theta$  ✓

$\sqrt{a^2 + x^2}$

$$x = a\tan\theta, \quad dx = a\sec^2\!\theta\,d\theta$$

$\tan\theta = \dfrac{x}{a}$  →  $\theta = \tan^{-1}\!\!\left(\dfrac{x}{a}\right)$

$a^2 + a^2\tan^2\!\theta = a^2\sec^2\!\theta$  ✓

$\sqrt{x^2 - a^2}$

$$x = a\sec\theta, \quad dx = a\sec\theta\tan\theta\,d\theta$$

$\sec\theta = \dfrac{x}{a}$  →  $\theta = \sec^{-1}\!\!\left(\dfrac{x}{a}\right)$

$a^2\sec^2\!\theta - a^2 = a^2\tan^2\!\theta$  ✓

Why the Triangle? — Converting Back to $x$

After integrating in $\theta$, you must substitute back to $x$. The triangle gives you every trig function of $\theta$ in terms of $x$ and $a$ — just read the sides!

$$\int\!\frac{dx}{\sqrt{a^2+x^2}} \;\xrightarrow{x=a\tan\theta}\; \int\!\frac{a\sec^2\!\theta\,d\theta}{a\sec\theta} = \int\sec\theta\,d\theta = \ln|\sec\theta+\tan\theta|+C$$
$$\begin{aligned} &\int\!\frac{dx}{\sqrt{a^2+x^2}} \;\xrightarrow{x=a\tan\theta} \\[4pt] &\int\sec\theta\,d\theta = \ln|\sec\theta\!+\!\tan\theta|+C \end{aligned}$$

Read from triangle: $\tan\theta = \frac{x}{a}$,  $\sec\theta = \frac{\sqrt{a^2+x^2}}{a}$. Substitute back:

$$= \ln\!\left|\frac{\sqrt{a^2+x^2}}{a}+\frac{x}{a}\right|+C = \ln\!\left|\sqrt{a^2+x^2}+x\right| + C'$$

Common Pitfall: Don't Forget to Substitute Back!

The #1 mistake with trig substitution is leaving the answer in $\theta$. Always use the triangle to convert every $\sin\theta$, $\cos\theta$, $\tan\theta$, etc. back to expressions in $x$.

$$\int\!\sqrt{4-x^2}\,dx = 2\theta + \sin 2\theta + C$$ Left answer in $\theta$ — incomplete!
$$= 2\sin^{-1}\!\frac{x}{2} + \frac{x\sqrt{4-x^2}}{2} + C$$ Substituted back using triangle ✓
