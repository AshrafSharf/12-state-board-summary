11.6   Bernoulli Distribution

Core Concept & Master Formula

A Bernoulli trial has exactly two outcomes: success ($X = 1$, probability $p$) and failure ($X = 0$, probability $q = 1 - p$).

$$f(x) = p^x (1-p)^{1-x}, \quad x = 0, 1$$
$$\boxed{\mu = p, \quad \sigma^2 = pq = p(1-p)}$$

When $p = q = \frac{1}{2}$: mean $= \frac{1}{2}$, variance $= \frac{1}{4}$.

CDF of Bernoulli

$$F(x) = \begin{cases} 0 & x < 0 \\ q = 1-p & 0 \le x < 1 \\ 1 & x \ge 1 \end{cases}$$

Spot the Clue

  • A single trial with two outcomes → Bernoulli
  • “Defective or not”, “pass or fail”, “heads or tails” for ONE trial
  • If it’s a single trial, it’s Bernoulli. If it’s $n$ repeated trials, it’s Binomial

Ex: $\text{Var}(2X - 3)$ where $X \sim \text{Ber}(0.4)$

Step 1: $\mu = p = 0.4$, so $q = 0.6$

Step 2: $\text{Var}(X) = pq = 0.4 \times 0.6 = 0.24$

Step 3: $\text{Var}(2X - 3) = 2^2 \cdot \text{Var}(X) = 4 \times 0.24$

$$\boxed{\text{Var}(2X - 3) = 0.96}$$

Watch Out

$$\text{Var}(2X - 3) = 4pq - 3$$ The constant $-3$ does NOT affect variance!
$$\text{Var}(2X - 3) = 4 \cdot pq$$ $\text{Var}(aX + b) = a^2 \text{Var}(X)$. The $b$ drops out.

Key: $\text{Bernoulli}(p) = B(1, p)$. The Binomial with $n = 1$ IS the Bernoulli distribution.
