11.5   Mathematical Expectation (Mean & Variance)

Core Concept & Master Formulas

Mean $E(X) = \mu$ measures central tendency. Variance $V(X) = \sigma^2$ measures spread.

$$\boxed{E(X) = \mu = \begin{cases} \sum_x x \cdot f(x) & \text{discrete} \\ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} x \cdot f(x)\,dx & \text{continuous} \end{cases}}$$
$$\boxed{\begin{aligned} &\text{Discrete: } E(X) = \sum_x x \cdot f(x) \\ &\text{Continuous: } E(X) = \int x \cdot f(x)\,dx \end{aligned}}$$
$$\boxed{V(X) = \sigma^2 = E(X^2) - [E(X)]^2}$$

Standard deviation: $\sigma = \sqrt{V(X)}$

Properties of $E$ and $V$

PropertyFormula
Linearity of $E$$E(aX + b) = aE(X) + b$
Scaling $E$$E(aX) = aE(X)$
Constant$E(b) = b$
Variance scaling$\text{Var}(aX + b) = a^2 \text{Var}(X)$
Constant variance$V(b) = 0$

Spot the Clue

  • “Find mean and variance” → build $\sum xf(x)$ and $\sum x^2 f(x)$ columns in a table
  • Shortcut $V(X) = E(X^2) - [E(X)]^2$ is almost always faster than $E(X - \mu)^2$
  • Given $E(X + 3) = 10$: use $E(X) + 3 = 10 \Rightarrow \mu = 7$
  • $\text{Var}(X + 3) = \text{Var}(X)$ — adding a constant does NOT change variance

Ex: Discrete — Find $c$, $E(X)$, $V(X)$

$f(x) = c^2, 2c^2, 3c^2, 4c^2, c, 2c$ for $x = 1,\ldots,6$.

$$10c^2 + 3c = 1 \implies c = \tfrac{1}{5}$$
$$E(X) = 4.6, \quad E(X^2) = 23.4$$
$$V(X) = 23.4 - (4.6)^2 = 2.24$$

Ex: Continuous — Exponential

$f(x) = \lambda e^{-\lambda x}$ for $x \ge 0$.

$$E(X) = \frac{1}{\lambda}$$
$$E(X^2) = \frac{2}{\lambda^2}$$
$$V(X) = \frac{2}{\lambda^2} - \frac{1}{\lambda^2} = \frac{1}{\lambda^2}$$

Watch Out

$$E(X^2) = [E(X)]^2$$ These are DIFFERENT! $E(X^2) \ge [E(X)]^2$ always.
$$V(X) = E(X^2) - [E(X)]^2$$ Compute $E(X)$ and $E(X^2)$ separately, then subtract.
$$\text{Var}(aX + b) = a^2 V(X) + b$$ The constant $b$ does NOT affect variance!
$$\text{Var}(aX + b) = a^2 V(X)$$ Only the scaling factor $a$ matters (squared).

Trap: Variance is always $\ge 0$. A negative result means a computational error.
