11.7   Binomial Distribution

Core Concept & Master Formulas

$X$ = number of successes in $n$ independent Bernoulli trials, each with constant probability $p$. Then $X \sim B(n, p)$.

$$\boxed{P(X = x) = \binom{n}{x} p^x q^{n-x}, \quad q = 1-p, \quad x = 0, 1, \ldots, n}$$
$$\boxed{P(X\!=\!x) = \binom{n}{x} p^x q^{n-x}}$$
$$\boxed{\mu = np, \quad \sigma^2 = npq = np(1-p)}$$

Conditions for Binomial

  • The $n$ trials are independent and $n$ is finite
  • Each trial has only two outcomes: success or failure
  • Probability of success $p$ remains constant across trials
$$P(X \ge 1) = 1 - q^n$$
$$P(X = 0) = q^n$$

Spot the Clue

  • “Repeated independent trials”, “tossed $n$ times”, “selected at random” with two outcomes
  • “Exactly $k$ out of $n$” → $P(X = k)$ with Binomial
  • Given mean and variance, find $n$ and $p$: $q = \frac{\text{Var}}{\text{Mean}} = \frac{npq}{np}$, then $p = 1-q$, then $n = \frac{\mu}{p}$
  • “At least one” = $1 - P(X = 0)$; “at most one” = $P(X=0) + P(X=1)$

Ex: From Mean & Variance, Find the Distribution

$np = 2$, $npq = 1.5$

$$q = \frac{1.5}{2} = \frac{3}{4}, \; p = \frac{1}{4}, \; n = 8$$
$$P(X\!=\!0) = \left(\frac{3}{4}\right)^{\!8}$$
$$P(X\!=\!1) = 8 \cdot \frac{1}{4}\left(\frac{3}{4}\right)^{\!7} = 2\!\left(\frac{3}{4}\right)^{\!7}$$
$$P(X \ge 1) = 1 - \left(\frac{3}{4}\right)^{\!8}$$

Ex: 20% Defective, Sample of 6

$n = 6$, $p = \frac{1}{5}$, $q = \frac{4}{5}$

$$P(X\!=\!2) = \binom{6}{2}\!\left(\frac{1}{5}\right)^{\!2}\!\left(\frac{4}{5}\right)^{\!4}$$
$$P(X \le 1) = \left(\frac{4}{5}\right)^{\!6} + 6\!\cdot\!\frac{1}{5}\!\left(\frac{4}{5}\right)^{\!5} = 2\!\left(\frac{4}{5}\right)^{\!5}$$
$$P(X \le 1) = 2\!\left(\frac{4}{5}\right)^{\!5}$$
$$P(X \ge 2) = 1 - 2\!\left(\frac{4}{5}\right)^{\!5}$$

Watch Out

$$P(X = 3) = p^3 q^{n-3}$$ Forgot $\binom{n}{3}$! Must count the arrangements.
$$P(X = 3) = \binom{n}{3} p^3 q^{n-3}$$ The $\binom{n}{x}$ counts ways to place $x$ successes among $n$ trials.

Trap: Binomial requires independence. Drawing WITHOUT replacement from a small population violates this. Drawing WITH replacement or from a large population is OK.
