11.8   Counting Principles & Choosing the Right Formula

Core Concept

Counting techniques determine sample space sizes, inverse image sizes, and the number of favorable outcomes in probability distribution problems.

$$\boxed{\binom{n}{r} = \frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!}} \quad \text{(selection, order doesn't matter)}$$
$$P(n,r) = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!}$$Arrangement, order matters
$$n^r$$$r$ choices from $n$ options, with repetition
$$2^n$$Binary choices for $n$ items

Decision Guide

  • “How many total outcomes?” — coins $\to 2^n$, dice $\to 6^k$, general $\to n^r$
  • “How many ways to choose/select?” (no arrangement) — $\binom{n}{r}$. Key words: choose, select, pick, committee
  • “How many ways to arrange/order?” — $P(n,r)$. Key words: arrange, sequence, rank
  • Binary decision for each item? — pass/fail for $n$ students $\to 2^n$
  • In Binomial pmf, $\binom{n}{x}$ counts ways to place $x$ successes among $n$ trials

Watch Out

$$\text{Choose 3 from 10} = P(10,3) = 720$$ Used permutations, but order doesn’t matter in selection!
$$\text{Choose 3 from 10} = \binom{10}{3} = 120$$ Selection = combinations. $P(n,r) = r! \cdot \binom{n}{r}$.
$$\text{2 dice outcomes} = \binom{6}{2} = 15$$ Dice produce ORDERED pairs, not combinations!
$$\text{2 dice outcomes} = 6^2 = 36$$ Each die is distinguishable. Use $n^r$ for ordered choices with repetition.

Trap: Choosing “exactly 2 red from 3 red and 2 white” uses the product $\binom{3}{2}\binom{2}{1}$ (hypergeometric-style counting).
